Color calculation

Each hour's color is determined by assessing each weather parameter individually (percent of precipitation, temperature, and wind speed), to get one of three desirability ratings - Low, Medium, and High - based on a simple equation. Here is a nerdy description of how that's decided, based on ranges. Below that is a more human-friendly translation: 
    precip: [0, 10]: High   [11, 30]: Medium  [31, ...]: Low
    temp:   [..., 32]: Low  [33, 41]: Medium  [42, 70]: High  [71, 85]: Medium    [86, ...]: Low
    wind:   [0,  8]: High   [ 9, 12]: Medium  [13, ...]: Low
  • Percent of precipitation: If the % is between 0 and 10% (inclusive) then the desirability is High. If the % is between 11% and 30% then desirability is Medium, and anything above 31% has a Low desirability.
  • Temperature: Anything up to 32°F has a Low desirability, 33°F to 41°F is Medium, temperatues between 42°F and 70°F are considered High desirability, then the desirability goes back down as the temperatures are higher.
  • Wind: Like percent of precipitation, the wind has three ranges, where lower is better.
